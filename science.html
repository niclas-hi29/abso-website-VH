The delivery science

The dose on the box,
delivered.

Most supplements are sold on what's in the bottle. The harder question is how much of it ever reaches your blood. Here's the physiology — and why a strip changes the answer.

Scroll
01 · The problem

Your liver taxes
the dose.

0%

Swallow a capsule and it travels through your gut to the liver before it ever reaches circulation — first-pass metabolism. For many compounds, only ~35% of the labelled dose survives that journey. The rest is broken down before it can do anything. The number on the panel and the number in your blood are two very different numbers.

02 · The shortcut

Under your
tongue.

0%

The mucosa under your tongue is one of the most permeable surfaces in the body, fed by a dense capillary bed that drains straight into systemic circulation — bypassing the gut and the liver entirely. Delivered this way, the same compounds reach roughly 70–90% bioavailability. It's the same physiology behind clinical sublingual films and why B12 is routinely given under the tongue.
